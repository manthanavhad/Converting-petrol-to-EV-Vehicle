Introduction

So, you've been thinking about switching to an electric car, whether to save the environment or to cut down on high fuel costs. But if you don’t have the budget to pick up a new EV, you might want to consider converting your existing petrol or diesel car to an electric vehicle. On this World Environment Day, let us look at the complexities, costs, and considerations involved in converting an internal combustion car (ICE) vehicle to an EV.

What is an EV Conversion Kit?
Conversion kits transform petrol or diesel vehicles into electric vehicles by replacing the internal combustion engine with an electric motor, battery pack, and controller. These components store and regulate energy, allowing the vehicle to operate like a standard electric vehicle.
Benefits:
1. Lower operational costs.
2. New life to older cars.
3. Environment friendly.
4. Lower maintenance costs.
Factors to consider before conversion:
1. Legality of the procedure.
2. Type and age of the vehicle.
3. Battery range and power requirements.
Process:
Converting a vehicle to electric involves replacing ICE components with an electric motor, battery pack, power controller, and charging system. Additional components are required, and RTO re-registration is necessary.
Cost:
Converting an ICE vehicle to EV costs Rs 4-6 lakh, depending on the conversion kit, additional materials, and professional labor charges, all of which vary by vehicle specifications and complexity.
Other costs:
Additional costs may include RTO fees, permit fees, inspection fees, insurance adjustments, and modifications to meet safety and regulatory standards.
